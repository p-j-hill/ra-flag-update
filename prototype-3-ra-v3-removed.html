The NHS has a duty to provide equal access to all health and care services – as stated by the Equality Act (2010). Healthcare staff must make reasonable adjustments to ensure health and care services are accessible to people with physical and/or mental impairments. A Reasonable Adjustment flag can show the potential adjustments for a patient, such as communication needs (supported by the Accessibility Standard). It may also show the patient’s impairments to help organisations understand the adjustments required.


The purpose of the Reasonable Adjustment flag is to ensure patients with physical and mental impairments have equal and fair access to healthcare services. This information is shared nationally with organisations providing direct care across the NHS. This allows patients to access the same levels of service across the country.

Please ensure it is entirely appropriate for the patient to have a Reasonable Adjustment before creating a flag. The patient must have a physical or mental impairment with substantial or long-term negative effects on their ability to do normal daily activities.

Healthcare professionals must obtain consent before being able to add, edit or remove a Reasonable Adjustment flag. Consent may be provided by the patient, their legal proxy or alternatively, through a best interest process. Obtaining and managing consent for creating and updating the flag follows existing national guidance and local processes for recording consent and making best interest decisions.
